Measurement caveat: TTD has no lead-level conversion tracking

The Trade Desk reports zero leads for this window because Cloudflare deployed only the universal pixel, not the per-event image pixels we supplied. The universal pixel fires on any page load and can only count generic site visits — it cannot isolate a lead-form submission. Lead-attribution analysis is treated separately in “Why lead volume looks low” below.

What TTD is actually measuring — and why it isn't leads

TTD's universal pixel credits site visits via view-through + click attribution. Cloudflare's CRM sees only post-click leads arriving on TTD-tagged URLs. The two systems answer different questions, so even site-visit numbers won't reconcile — and lead numbers can't reconcile at all until the image pixels go live.

Universal pixel (deployed)

Sitewide tag, fires on every page load.

Counts a user as “converted” if they touch any Cloudflare URL after seeing/clicking a TTD ad. Catches site visits — cannot split leads from non-lead traffic.

Image pixels (supplied, not deployed)

Per-event tags, placed on specific pages (e.g. lead-form thank-you).

Fire only when a user lands on the conversion page. Would isolate actual leads from background traffic, by ad set / region / creative. We supplied these — client opted not to implement.

Recommendation: deploy the supplied image pixels on the SASE lead-form thank-you page. Until then, TTD performance has to be judged on the upper-funnel signals it does measure cleanly: impressions, reach, clicks, CTR, CPC.

Likely culprit on click → submit drop: required Work phone number

The SASE 10-Steps form has two required fields and pre-fills the rest from the LinkedIn profile:

  • Work email * — required, expected for a whitepaper
  • Work phone number * — required and the friction point
  • First name, last name, country, job title, company — auto-filled, low friction

B2B prospects associate giving a phone number with sales calls. For a top-of-funnel downloadable guide, that mental tax is high enough to abandon. Recommend: drop "Work phone number" to optional, or remove it

Why lead volume looks low — the four-part story

Headline measured leads under-represent reality. Here is what is actually happening, ordered by impact.
1

TTD has no lead-level pixel

Cloudflare deployed only the universal pixel, which counts generic site visits and can't isolate a lead-form completion. We supplied per-event image pixels for the lead-form thank-you page; the client opted not to implement them. Until they're live, TTD-driven leads are literally unmeasurable — not low, just invisible.

2

The mix is upper-funnel by design

Reading the campaign naming convention, ~78% of spend sits in MOFU/awareness ad sets (AWARENESS, AWR-CONS, SHADOW-AI TOFU). Only the BOFU_RETARGETING_LEAD-GEN slices are optimized for form fills — lead volume should be modest by construction.

3

Reddit is on a TOFU goal, not Lead

$2.4K of Reddit spend ran on SHADOW-AI awareness ad sets with goal “-” (no conversion goal set). Zero leads is on plan for that placement; only the small APAC-ANZ retargeting line was configured for LEAD goal and it received negligible delivery.

4

LinkedIn form is the bottleneck

Tracked LinkedIn leads vs clicks indicate ~1.0% click-to-lead. The form requires Work phone number alongside Work email — the rest of the fields are auto-filled. That single required phone field is the likely cause: prospects assume it triggers a sales call.
